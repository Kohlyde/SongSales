Introduction to CPTSD and Combat PTSD

Definition: Complex PTSD (CPTSD) results from prolonged exposure to traumatic events, often involving harm or abandonment by a caregiver. Combat PTSD is experienced by military personnel due to traumatic events in combat. Symptoms include re-experiencing trauma, avoidance, negative changes in thoughts and mood, and heightened arousal and reactivity.

Symptoms and Daily Struggles:

  • Night Sweats and Inability to Sleep: Nightmares and night sweats disrupt sleep patterns, leading to chronic fatigue. (Van Der Kolk, 2014)
  • Cold Sweats: Physical reactions to stress or memories of the trauma. (Herman, 1992)
  • Outbursts: Emotional dysregulation leading to sudden and intense outbursts of anger or frustration. (APA, 2013)
  • Hypervigilance: Constantly being on high alert can be exhausting and contribute to anxiety. (Smith et al., 2020)
  • Triggers: Everyday situations can trigger intense memories, causing significant distress. (Van Der Kolk, 2014)

Impact on Daily Life:

  • Personal Relationships: Emotional volatility strains relationships, leading to isolation. (Herman, 1992)
  • Work and Professional Life: Concentration issues and absenteeism impact job performance. (APA, 2013)
  • Physical Health: Chronic pain and immune system suppression due to constant stress. (Smith et al., 2020)
Introduction to ADHD Combined Type

Definition: ADHD Combined Type is characterized by a combination of inattentive and hyperactive-impulsive symptoms. Symptoms include difficulty sustaining attention, disorganization, procrastination, restlessness, impulsive decisions, and frequent interruptions.

Symptoms and Daily Struggles:

  • Difficulty Sustaining Attention: Challenges in focusing on tasks for extended periods. (Barkley, 2014)
  • Disorganization: Chronic disorganization leads to misplaced items and missed appointments. (Brown, 2005)
  • Procrastination: Tendency to procrastinate leads to last-minute rushes and suboptimal performance. (Barkley, 2014)
  • Restlessness: Constant need to move, fidget, or tap. (Brown, 2005)
  • Impulsive Decisions: Hasty decisions without considering consequences. (Barkley, 2014)
  • Interrupting Others: Frequent interruptions in conversations. (Brown, 2005)

Impact on Daily Life:

  • Personal Relationships: Misunderstandings and conflicts with friends and family. (Barkley, 2014)
  • Work and Professional Life: Performance issues and job instability. (Brown, 2005)
  • Physical Health: Sleep disorders and high levels of stress and anxiety. (Barkley, 2014)
Introduction to Twice Exceptional (2e) Individuals

Definition: Twice exceptional individuals are both gifted and have a learning disability. They possess high intellectual capabilities but face challenges in areas like reading, writing, or social interactions.

Symptoms and Daily Struggles:

  • High Intellectual Abilities: Exceptional problem-solving skills and creativity. (Baum & Owen, 2004)
  • Rapid Learning: Ability to grasp complex concepts quickly. (Foley Nicpon et al., 2011)
  • Dyslexia: Difficulties in reading and writing. (Baum & Owen, 2004)
  • Social Challenges: Struggles with social interactions and forming relationships. (Foley Nicpon et al., 2011)

Impact on Daily Life:

  • Personal Relationships: Social isolation and misunderstandings. (Baum & Owen, 2004)
  • Work and Professional Life: Underachievement and job dissatisfaction. (Foley Nicpon et al., 2011)
  • Physical Health: Stress and increased risk of anxiety and depression. (Baum & Owen, 2004)
